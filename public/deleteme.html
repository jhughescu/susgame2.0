A number of recent surveys have highlighted the importance for students to have relevant, up-to-date digital skills both for study and for employability. To help you to enhance your digital skills during your time at Cranfield we provide a self-assessment tool to discover how your skills compare against the six elements of the Jisc Building digital capabilities framework.

To support your development and fill any skills gaps identified we offer a range of learning opportunities and resources. This includes:

  • a rolling programme of free online or classroom-based courses, working with software applications and University tools and templates, with the support of a professional trainer;
  • self-paced resources including online learning tools and video tutorials, such as our CITBytes YouTube channel and free access to LinkedIn Learning;
  • access to professional digital skills qualifications, such as Microsoft Fundamentals Professional Certifications and the ICDL - International Certification of Digital Literacy (fees apply), alongside your degree award; 
  • one-to-one support.

Further details will be available to you once you have completed the registration process.

Digital skills awareness - Free online course

During your studies you will use a range of digital tools and applications. If you are returning to study after a break or you are new to studying in the UK, we encourage you to you take the Digital Skills Awareness for Starting Higher Education course hosted on the FutureLearn platform. It was developed by Bloomsbury Learning Exchange (BLE), an organisation dedicated to sharing digital education expertise.

Please be aware that the course is free to use on the FutureLearn platform provided you choose the option to ‘Join with limited access’ - you will see the option at the bottom of the purchasing options in the right hand column.

What will I learn?

During this 3 week course you will find out about skills to support your learning:

  • Week 1 - Your learning environment
    • Expectations of HE, accessing online learning, online learning environments, and assignments and assessment.
  • Week 2 - Foundations of digital study
    • Digital study tools, written communication, the internet, and working with files.
  • Week 3 - Digital safety and wellbeing
    • Safety and security, social media in a learning context, digital wellbeing, time management.

LinkedIn Learning

Develop your current skills further, learn something completely new or delve into a passion you never thought you could with LinkedIn Learning, the premium online learning platform. All students have free access to the wide range of online learning resources in LinkedIn Learning, and it covers so much more than digital skills.

To find out more about LinkedIn Learning, view our short video (2 minutes 4 seconds).
